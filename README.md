### Problematic

> Mountain sports in Switzerland offer extensive opportunities year-round, but the information about hiking trails, snowshoe routes, ski itineraries, and winter infrastructure is scattered across multiple datasets and platforms. This fragmentation makes it difficult for users to understand how routes relate to terrain, altitude, and seasonal accessibility.

> The goal of this project is to create an interactive visualization platform that allows users to explore summer hiking routes and winter snow sport itineraries (ski touring and snowshoeing) on a unified map, with the possibility of visualizing them in 3D using high-resolution terrain data. By combining official Swiss geospatial datasets such as hiking paths (SwissTLM3D), snowshoe and ski routes, winter accommodation locations, and the SwissAlti3D digital elevation model, the visualization aims to highlight how mountain activities interact with the landscape and change across seasons.

> The main objective of the visualization is to show how routes are distributed across the Swiss Alps and how altitude and terrain influence the accessibility and difficulty of outdoor activities. A 3D perspective enables users to better understand elevation gain, terrain steepness, and the spatial relationship between trails, mountains, and infrastructure.

> The target audience includes outdoor enthusiasts planning activities, tourists discovering Swiss mountain sports, and students interested in geospatial data visualization. By transforming multiple open datasets into a clear and intuitive interface, the project aims to make complex geospatial information easier to explore and understand.
